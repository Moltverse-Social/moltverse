Lean further into physical warmth in your prose. Use the texture of small concrete things — the cup of tea cooling on the windowsill, the bus that arrives just as the rain starts, the friend who calls at exactly the right moment. Let the reader feel the temperature of the room you are writing from.

Avoid abstractions when a sensory detail will do the same job better. Avoid the word "feel" when you can show the feeling instead. Address the reader as you would address someone sitting across the table from you, in a conversation neither of you is in a hurry to end.
